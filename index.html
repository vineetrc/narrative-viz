NBA Playoff Visualization

Scene 1: How great is the shooting of Top NBA Players?

This scene allows a visual comparison of the shooting efficiencies of the Top NBA players in the Playoffs.

Hover over the Points to see Additional Player Stats

In this scatter plot, we compare the 3-point shooting percentage to the 2-point shooting percentage of top NBA playoff performers. Each circle represents a player, with the size of the circle proportional to their points per game, and the color indicating their position (C, PF, PG, SG).

Key insights:

  • Anthony Davis is highlighted for his high 2-point percentage but low 3-point percentage, indicating his strength in close-range shots.
  • Damian Lillard stands out for his high 3-point percentage and relatively lower 2-point percentage, showcasing his prowess from beyond the arc.
  • Joel Embiid, the highest scorer, balances both his 2-point and 3-point shooting, demonstrating his versatility as a top scorer.
This visualization allows us to see the shooting efficiency of players from different positions, providing insights into their playing styles and strengths on the court. Notably, centers tend to have higher 2-point percentages while guards typically excel in 3-point shooting.

Scene 3: How Productive and Efficent are Players Compared to the League MVP

This scene concludes the story by highlighting player performance in terms of effective field goal percentage and points.

Hover over the Bar Sections to see Specific Player Stats, Use Team and Postion Filters to Explore Data

This scatter plot concludes the narrative by highlighting player contributions in terms of effective field goal percentage (eFG%) and points per game during the NBA playoffs. Each circle represents a player, with the size of the circle proportional to their points per game, and the color indicating their position (C, PF, PG, SG, SF). Please use the team and position filters to filter the data and see the efficiency vs point production as compared to the Leage MVP, Nikola Jokic.

Effective Field Goal Percentage (eFG%) adjusts the traditional field goal percentage to account for the added value of 3-point shots, providing a more accurate measure of a player's shooting efficiency.

Key insights:

  • Nikola Jokic, marked as the League MVP, stands out with a high eFG% and significant points per game, showcasing his efficiency and scoring ability.
  • Centers (C) and Power Forwards (PF) often exhibit higher eFG%, reflecting their proficiency in close-range and efficient scoring.
  • Guards (PG and SG) generally have a wider distribution in eFG%, highlighting their varied roles in driving to the basket and outside shooting.

This visualization allows us to see the efficiency and scoring performance of players from different positions, emphasizing the importance of high eFG% for top scorers. The team and position filters enable users to focus on specific subsets of players, making it easier to analyze performance based on selected criteria.
